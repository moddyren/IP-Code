What is IP Code?

Understanding IP Ratings

IP Code (Ingress Protection Code) is an international standard system that classifies and rates the degree of protection provided by mechanical casings and electrical enclosures against intrusion, dust, accidental contact, and water.

IP Code Structure: IP XX

First Digit (0-6)

Protection against solid objects and dust

Second Digit (0-9)

Protection against liquid ingress

First Digit - Solid Protection

Level Protection Description
IP0X No Protection No protection against contact and ingress of objects
IP1X ≥50mm Protection against solid objects over 50mm
IP2X ≥12.5mm Protection against solid objects over 12.5mm
IP3X ≥2.5mm Protection against solid objects over 2.5mm
IP4X ≥1.0mm Protection against solid objects over 1.0mm
IP5X Dust Protected Protection against dust limited ingress
IP6X Dust Tight Total protection against dust

What is IP Code Test Equipment?

Understanding IP Code Testing

IP (Ingress Protection) Code Test Equipment is essential for evaluating and certifying the protection levels of electrical enclosures. These specialized tools help manufacturers ensure their products meet international safety standards.

Protection Against Solid Objects

Tests resistance to dust, debris, and accidental contact using standardized probes ranging from 50mm to 1mm.

Water Ingress Testing

Verifies protection against water exposure through various methods including dripping, spraying, and immersion tests.

Common IP Ratings Explained

Rating Protection Level
IP65 Dust-tight and protected against water jets
IP67 Dust-tight and protected against temporary immersion
IP68 Dust-tight and protected against continuous immersion

IP1X/2X/3X/4X Test Probes

IP Test Probe Kit is a set of tools used to determine the degrees of protection provided by product enclosure against access to hazardous parts and ingress of solid foreign objects. lt consists of access probes and object probes, which are required to be pushed against or inserted through any openings of the enclosure with the specified forces during testing. The access probes are used to simulate in a conventional manner a part of a person or a tool held by an operator to confirm whether there is sufficient clearance from hazardous parts. The object probes are applied to simulate solid foreign objects to confirm the possibility of ingress into an enclosure.

IP1X test probe - 50.0 mm diameter sphere

Used to test protection against access with the back of hand or solid foreign objects. The sphere probe has a diameter of 50mm, complying with IEC60529 standard requirements.

Water Ingress Testing Equipment

IPX1/IPX2 Drip Test

Equipment designed for testing protection against vertically falling water drops and tilted drops up to 15°. Simulates exposure to rain and dripping water.

IPX3/IPX4 Spray Test

Oscillating tube and spray nozzle apparatus for testing protection against water spraying and splashing from multiple angles.

IPX5/IPX6 Jet Test

High-pressure water jet testing equipment to verify protection against water jets from any direction. Uses standardized nozzles and controlled pressure.

IPX7/IPX8 Immersion Test

Immersion tanks and depth control systems for temporary and continuous immersion testing. Ensures water-tight sealing at specified depths.
